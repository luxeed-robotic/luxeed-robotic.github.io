Promoting the Transition to Organic and Regenerative Weeding

Weeding isn’t just about killing weeds around crops: it’s about the future of our soil.

LUXEED Robotics is turning weeding into a regenerative and profitable business. Slashing costs in the millions, saving crops, doubling farmer profits on the same plot to produce more food for the ever-growing global population. No herbicides. No soil disruption.

Farmers have battled weeds for centuries, watching their profits shrink, soils degrade, and herbicide resistance thrive. Now, they finally have a better way: lasers and AI.

LUXEED is changing the game of laserweeding: transforming it from a massive, slow, and energy-hungry process meant for large farms only, into a fast and lightweight solution accessible for all.

And we’re doing it while restoring soil health. Soil is a living carbon sink that, when left undisturbed, can capture as much CO₂ as taking over 1 billion cars off the road every year. Weeding is a destructive, necessary farm task standing in the way of regeneration and future carbon credits for the farmers.

SDG 2 Zero Hunger - Target 2.4

The implement enhances the efficiency of agricultural practices by using a high-precision laser weeding system. This technology controls competition between crops and weeds for essential resources (nutrients, light, and water) ensuring agricultural productivity, therefore food security by sustainable and resilient agriculture. For example, onion crops have very poor competitive ability with weeds due to its inherent characteristics like short stature, non-branching habit, sparse foliage, shallow root system and extremely slow growth during initial stage. Yield losses for onion plots due to weed competition can be as high as 82.2% (Tewari et al., 2003).

SDG 6 Clear Water and Sanitation - Target 6.3

Traditional agricultural methods often involve the use of herbicides, compromising the environmental ecosystem and endangering human health. According to the European Water Agency (8), herbicide concentration above standards levels were found in up to 25% of surface water monitoring sites across Europe. The standard levels reflect the need to keep concentrations of herbicides as low as possible in ground water to avoid human health risk. LUXEED's aims to eliminate the use of herbicides, and their residues in products, the soil, and the risk of contamination of water bodies.

SDG 8 Decent Work and Economic Growth - Target 8.2

Currently, the EU faces acute personnel shortages, which is a limiting factor for the horticulture industry, more so with the availability of workforce during peak harvesting seasons (9). By automating the labor- intensive process of weeding, the implement not only improves efficiency but also enhances job quality by allowing agricultural workers to engage in more productive tasks. The development and implementation of such advanced agricultural technologies can stimulate economic growth through new technological markets and employment opportunities in the agri-tech sector.

SDG 13 Climate Action - Target 13.1

Open-field crops are increasingly vulnerable to the adverse effects of climate change, with extreme weather events becoming more frequent. LUXEED’s lightweight design minimizes soil compaction, preserving its hydraulic conductivity and carrying capacity of water (10). Additionally, laser weeding prevents disturbance to the soil, maintaining the integrity of the soil structure. As severe weather events—such as heavy rainfall and prolonged droughts—continue to threaten crop yields, protecting the soil’s ability to infiltrate and retain water is essential in mitigating the impacts of climate change.

SDG 15 Life On Land - Target 15.1

Herbicide application (11) can have indirect consequences of terrestrial ecosystems due to spray drift and erosion by wind and water. Up to 25% (12) of applied pesticides are carried by air currents, leading to spread across hundreds of kilometers. LUXEED eliminates the need for herbicide usage while also minimizing soil disruption and non-target species, preserving soil health and biodiversity.
